On moles in the lawn
Back lawn · this morning
Something's absolutely wrecking the lawn! Little volcano mounds all over. Not brilliant.
Moles, I'm afraid. The velvet menaces. Your lawn's the healthy one on the street, by the way. They only bother with gardens where the worms are worth the effort. Bit backhanded as compliments go.
Flattering. Now how do I evict them?
Castor oil granules are your best bet. Scatter them along the runs and water them in well. Moles loathe the taste and tend to relocate within a week or two. Kinder than traps, and you won't have to look at the evidence.
Brilliant. How long before I can actually use the lawn again?
Give it a fortnight for them to clear off, then rake the mounds flat and fill the dips with a sandy topsoil mix. Fresh grass seed on top and you'll hardly know they were here. I'll remind you at the end of April to check for fresh activity.
Thanks! You're oddly calming about moles.
They're just gardeners with a different method. We'll sort it.
On a mystery crop
Dug over the bed where the spuds were last year. Found about two kilos of potatoes I definitely didn't plant. Edible?
Volunteer potatoes! The ones that got missed at harvest and just got on with it. Best kind of free lunch.
Are they safe to eat? Bit worried about blight.
Yes, assuming none of them have gone green or started sprouting like they've got plans. Bin any that have. One quick catch though: volunteers can carry blight over from last year, so don't plant new seed potatoes in that bed this season. Rotate to beans, they'll love it.
Beans it is. Any varieties you'd push me towards?
For Sheffield clay, 'Cobra' French beans are reliable and don't mind a damp summer. If you fancy something prettier, 'Painted Lady' runners look like they belong in a watercolour. I'll nudge you when it's warm enough to sow.
Perfect. One last thing, what do I do with the potatoes in the meantime? Paper bag? Fridge?
Somewhere cool, dark and dry. A paper bag in the cupboard under the stairs is ideal. Not the fridge (too cold, they'll go sweet). Use them within a month or so and you'll be having proper homegrown roasties by Sunday.
